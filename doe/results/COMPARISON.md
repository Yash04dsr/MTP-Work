## 4. Key takeaways

1. **Area vs mass-flux vs volume-flux CoV** differ by ~3 % on the coarse
   case and ~6‚Äď9 % on the medium case. The mass-flux weighting is slightly
   lower (the plume aligns somewhat with the main flow), which is physically
   sensible ‚ÄĒ the hydrogen rides along the faster central streamlines.

2. **CoV-on-time-averaged field** (1a) is consistently **~25 % lower** than
   the mean-of-per-snapshot CoV (1b) because the latter is inflated by
   acoustic density fluctuations. Section 1a is the correct "mixing"
   metric; Section 1b only reflects instantaneous snapshot variability.

3. **Medium mesh gives higher CoV than coarse** (0.20 vs 0.14 area-weighted)
   ‚ÄĒ the finer mesh better resolves the H‚āā plume and does *not*
   numerically-diffuse it, so the genuine segregation is captured. This
   confirms the mesh-independence conclusion that the coarse mesh was
   over-smoothing the concentration field.

4. **Clean area-weighted őĒP**: coarse = 10.4 kPa, medium = 4.3 kPa. The
   medium mesh shows **lower pressure drop**, consistent with less numerical
   diffusion and less over-prediction of secondary losses.

5. **Mass balance from the clean `outletFlux` time series** is +68 kg/s
   (coarse) and +71 kg/s (medium), matching the analytical mass inflow of
   ~66 kg/s within 3 ‚Äď 7 % ‚ÄĒ this is the time-averaged physical balance.
   The per-snapshot balance (Section 3b) is *not* representative because
   each snapshot catches an acoustic phase.

6. **Danckwerts I_s** (intensity of segregation): small (~1‚Äď2 √ó 10‚ĀĽ¬≥) because
   ‚ü®Y_H‚āā‚ü© is small (~3 %). I_s saturates near zero for fine mixing and is
   more sensitive than CoV for very well-mixed streams. Use I_s when
   comparing across cases with different ‚ü®Y_H‚āā‚ü© (the upcoming DoE).
